My name is Giang (spelled like Zhang), and I am a rising junior at Colorado College, majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics.

When I first arrived at the college, I didn't have an idea of what I wanted to major in. Being young, wild, and naive, I was fixated on traveling and learning a bit of everything. Due to the flexible nature of Colorado College's block plan, I took a block off to join a month-long coding challenge in California. Never in my life have I ever felt so hooked: because the program's language was C, I got the chance to learn about pointers, memory allocation/collection, and to solve fundamental problems without libraries. I left yearning for more!

The experience opened a new door in my life. Coming from Vietnam -- a somewhat conservative, male-dominated country, I never thought I could be a software engineer. In my mind, I was not it: I wasn't the best kid in STEM, nor did I find myself fit the narrative of a tech-savvy person. Despite my growing doubts, I found myself lucky to have the most supportive of friends, who reassured me of my decision. At the end of my sophomore year, I finally had the gut to follow the major.
